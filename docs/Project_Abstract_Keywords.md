Abstract and Keywords for Statistical Research on Bilge Water Oil Pollution
Abstract
This statistical research aims to validate the estimated annual volume of 2.3 trillion liters of oil-contaminated bilge water discharged globally from small vessels under 400 gross tonnage. The theoretical framework focuses on establishing a quantitative methodology by collecting and analyzing publicly available data on three core parameters:
(1) global fleet size and distribution of recreational and small commercial vessels exempt from MARPOL oil-water separator requirements,
(2) average bilge water generation rates per vessel type based on operational characteristics and engine configurations,
(3) The identification and scientifically justified delimitation of suitable chemical parameters for the analysis of oil- and detergent-related contamination. Particular consideration will be given to total petroleum hydrocarbons (TPH/MKW), potentially supplemented by polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), aliphatic and aromatic hydrocarbon fractions, surfactants (anionic and non-ionic), as well as aggregate organic parameters (e.g., TOC, DOC, COD).
(4) The structured analysis of national and international water quality databases (e.g., ELWAS-Web, UBA, BfG, HELCOM, WISE) as well as relevant regional and port authority reports.
The research will utilize automated data scraping from public databases including maritime registration authorities, environmental monitoring agencies, and vessel activity records to compile fleet statistics, operational profiles, and discharge patterns.
By integrating these datasets with documented bilge generation rates and oil content measurements from scientific literature, the study will construct a comprehensive statistical model to verify or refine the 2.3 trillion liter estimate, thereby quantifying the environmental burden addressed by gravity-separation bilge water treatment systems for small vessels.
